Common Questions

Everything you need to know

Is CanHOA legal in my state?
Yes. CanHOA is designed to comply with HOA laws in all 50 states. When you set up your community, you select your state and the platform automatically applies the correct notice periods, election rules, fine limits, and meeting requirements. We update these rules whenever state laws change.
Who holds our HOA's money?
Your HOA holds its own money — always. CanHOA never touches your funds. We use Stripe and Plaid to securely connect your existing HOA bank account. Dues paid by residents go directly into your HOA's account. We charge our platform fee separately via your community's card on file.
What's the cost for online payment processing?
ACH bank transfers (the most common payment method) cost $0.80 per transaction, flat — much lower than credit cards. Credit/debit card payments are 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction (standard Stripe rate). You can choose whether to pass the processing fee to the resident or absorb it as a community expense.
Can we import our existing data?
Yes. You can import unit owner data from a CSV spreadsheet, upload existing documents (PDF, Word, Excel), and migrate financial history. For Pro plan communities, our onboarding team handles the data migration for you at no extra charge.
Do residents need to download an app?
No app required — CanHOA works fully in any web browser on phones, tablets, and computers. Residents get a link to their community portal and can log in with their email. A dedicated iOS and Android app is on our roadmap for later this year.
What happens if we cancel?
You can export all your data — documents, financials, resident records, violation history — as downloadable files at any time. There are no lock-in contracts. Cancel month-to-month with 30 days notice. Your data is available for download for 90 days after cancellation.
